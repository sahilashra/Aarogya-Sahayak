CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Patient presents with increased thirst, frequent urination, and unexplained weight loss over the past 3 months.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
Patient reports drinking 4-5 liters of water daily and waking up 3-4 times at night to urinate. She has lost approximately 8 kg in the last 3 months despite maintaining normal appetite. - No previous diagnosis of diabetes
- No history of cardiovascular disease

HbA1c: 8.2% (normal <5.7%)

Kidney Function: Creatinine 0.9 mg/dL, eGFR >90 mL/min/1.73m²

ASSESSMENT:
Newly diagnosed Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus with poor glycemic control. Patient also has dyslipidemia and hypertension, increasing cardiovascular risk.

PLAN:
1. Initiate Metformin 500mg twice daily with meals, titrate up as tolerated
2. Dietary counseling - low glycemic index diet, reduce refined carbohydrates
3. Lifestyle modification - recommend 30 minutes of moderate exercise 5 days per week
4. Monitor blood glucose at home - fasting and 2 hours post-meal
5. Repeat HbA1c in 3 months to assess glycemic control
6. Ophthalmology referral for diabetic retinopathy screening
7. Continue Amlodipine for hypertension
8. Consider statin therapy for dyslipidemia management
9. Patient education on diabetes self-management, foot care, and hypoglycemia recognition
10. Follow-up in 2 weeks to assess medication tolerance and glucose control

PATIENT EDUCATION PROVIDED:
Discussed diabetes diagnosis, importance of medication adherence, dietary modifications, regular exercise, and monitoring for complications. Patient verbalized understanding and agreed to treatment plan.
