Patient presents for routine follow-up of hypertension and reports occasional headaches and dizziness.

Patient was diagnosed with essential hypertension 8 years ago. He reports good medication compliance but has been experiencing frontal headaches 2-3 times per week, especially in the morning. - No history of diabetes, stroke, or myocardial infarction

FAMILY HISTORY:

Non-smoker, occasional alcohol consumption (2-3 drinks per week), works as office administrator with minimal physical activity.

- Hydrochlorothiazide 12.5mg once daily

ASSESSMENT:
1. Uncontrolled essential hypertension despite dual therapy
2. Obesity (BMI 31.2)
3. Prediabetes
4. Dyslipidemia with low HDL
5. Increased cardiovascular risk

PLAN:
1. Increase Enalapril to 20mg once daily for better blood pressure control
2. Continue Hydrochlorothiazide 12.5mg once daily
3. Add Amlodipine 5mg once daily if blood pressure remains elevated after 2 weeks
4. Dietary counseling - DASH diet, reduce sodium intake to <2g per day
5. Weight reduction goal - lose 5-7% body weight over 6 months
6. Exercise prescription - 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week
7. Home blood pressure monitoring - twice daily for 1 week, then weekly
8. Repeat lipid profile and kidney function in 3 months
9. Screen for obstructive sleep apnea given obesity and hypertension
10. Follow-up in 2 weeks to assess blood pressure response and medication tolerance

PATIENT EDUCATION PROVIDED:
Discussed importance of blood pressure control to prevent stroke, heart attack, and kidney disease. Reviewed dietary modifications including reducing salt, increasing fruits and vegetables, and portion control. Emphasized need for regular physical activity and weight loss. Patient expressed motivation to make lifestyle changes and agreed to home blood pressure monitoring.
